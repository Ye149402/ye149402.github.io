Personal Process

From 1959 to 1968, he studied at Sein Pao High School (now Atka (6), Botahtaung). From 1968 to 1972, he transferred to Atka (1) Thingangyun School and graduated from the basic education high school. During his student life, he served as the secretary of the Literary Development Association and the joint secretary of the Science Association.

He attended Yangon University from 1972 to 1977 and received a Bachelor of Arts (Philosophy). He wrote poems under the pen name Taw Kyaw Monk for the university's wall newspaper and annual magazine. He worked in book publishing. He participated in student movements and was briefly detained.

In 1976, he entered the literary field with the short story "When the Sun Sets, the Moon Rises" published in the movie magazine Padesha.

He married Ms. San San Aung (a university library employee) in 1981, and they had two daughters, she and writer Yuya . In 1992, he began writing video and film scripts.

On September 26, 1989, he was sentenced to seven (7) years in prison, along with editor Myo Myint Nyein and publisher Ko Sein Hlaing, for writing a long poem entitled "What Has Happened?", which reflected on the post-1988 regime.

From 1991 to 1993, I was unable to write or publish any books.

n 2009, he began writing "Read" (book review section) for the Putur Teen Magazine under the pen name "Min Ko Nyan".

He lived with his family at his mother's house in Thaduyan, Thingangyun, and wrote plays, poems, essays, and screenplays. His published books were adapted into many film scripts, most of which he wrote himself.

End of life

Min Lu died of lung cancer at the age of 59 on August 14, 2013 at 11:30 p.m. at Victoria Hospital in Yangon.
